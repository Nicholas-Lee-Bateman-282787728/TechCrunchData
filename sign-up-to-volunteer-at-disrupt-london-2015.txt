Do you want to roll up your sleeves and get to work helping the TechCrunch Disrupt team put on the best startup show in Europe? If so, you should definitely sign up to be a Work Exchange Volunteer at Disrupt London 2015. Applications are now open.Volunteers selected for the Work Exchange must provide up to 16 hours of work during the show, where theyll do things like staff the registration booth, assemble VIP goodie bags, provide directions to attendees andhelp with the Hackathon presentations  basically everything that helps ensure attendees and presenters have an amazing experience at the show. Disrupt volunteers must be available Friday, December 4, at 4 p.m. at the Copper Box Arena for a mandatory orientation meeting, and available to work shifts between 6 a.m. on Saturday, December 5, and 10 p.m. on Tuesday, December 8.Volunteers get an all-access pass to the Disrupt London conference, so you can tour the show floor whenyoure not working a shift. There, youll be able to network with some of the most prominent entrepreneurs, investors and technology enthusiasts from around the world, plus youll get to connect and share learnings with the other amazing Work Exchange volunteers working alongside you. All volunteers will receive a goodie bag with a bunch of awesome TechCrunch swag.Those who are accepted into the program are required to cover their own meals, housing and travel expenses. There will likely be some opportunities for Work Exchange volunteers to share rooms, and well send along an invitation to the programs Slack page to all those selected so that you can connect with all the other Work Exchange volunteers ahead of time.You can apply to be a Work Exchange volunteer by filling out this brief form no later than Friday, November 20. Well notify those selected into the program via email shortly after applications close, and those selected will need to respond to the email to secure their spot in the program.

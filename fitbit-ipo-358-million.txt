Fitness tracker FitBit has updated the filing for its initial public offering with new pricing details.The company plans to sell just under 22.4 million shares priced between $14 to $16. At the high end, that would bring the total amount raised in its IPO to $358 million. The filing also states that FitBit stockholders plan to sell 7.5 million shares.FitBit will trade on the New York Stock Exchange under the symbol FIT.

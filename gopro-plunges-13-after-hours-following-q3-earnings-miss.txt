GoPro took a dive Wednesday after releasing Q3financials that disappointed street expectations.At the markets close, GoProreported a miss on itsQ3 earnings,postingan adjusted $0.25 per shareon $400.3 million non-GAAP revenue during the period. Those figures compared tostreet expectationsof a $0.29 per-share profit, and revenue of $433.6 million.The action camera makers $400.3 revenues represented a 43% year-over-year increase from $280.0 in Q3 2015, with EPS also up significantly from $0.12 in the corresponding quarterlast year. The company shipped 1.6 million camera devices in Q3, up 46% from Q3 2014, but still less than the street had expected.In a statement, GoPro CEOand founder Nicholas Woodmansaid business in the third quarter was clearly more difficult than anticipated.The company shipped a couple new devices this quarter including the Hero+ and Hero4 Session, and announcedplans to build a consumer drone.Interestingly, GoPro emphasized how important foreign markets, specifically China, had been to the companys growth. Sales outside of the U.S. reportedly made up more than 50% of the companys revenue. The company said China was the fastest growing market inGoProshistory.
